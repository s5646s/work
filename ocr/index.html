Improved a Review system in the Naver map
I addressed the business goal of boosting user engagement through showing more reviews from verified users and higher-quality reviews. To translate the business needs into a tangible design solution, I extensively explored various technology and design options.
As a result, the implemented design solution successfully achieved a significant 21% increase in screen views, and the number of screen views and number of users increased by 14% in just one week.

About the Receipt Review feature
NAVER believed that people who actually visited a place could provide the most reliable reviews, which reflects the service goal.
In November 2019, NAVER released a "Receipt Review" feature using OCR* technology.
Places that users have visited are exposed on the platform by simply scanning their receipt.
*OCR (optical character recognition) is a technology that can scan and save text from images.
Problem Statement
" How might I help users to accurately input information during the receipt verification process to boost user engagement?
In 2019, we tried to collect reliable reviews through receipts. But users weren't engaged and struggled to find trustworthy place reviews on our platform. As a result, they searched for reviews on other platforms. Our success metric was user engagement (receipt reviews).
Process
Solution
Provide clear guidelines for accurate scan results and a more personalized visit history for each user.

Initial Goal
Exploration
Step 1
Evaluate previous flow
The first step was to review the current flow of actions. The process of verifying the receipt had a low probability of completion due to multiple points of failure.
Users often missed necessary information while scanning receipts due to unclear instructions.

Users struggled to successfully scan receipts due to vague instructions, resulting in missed crucial details or mismatched information.
Users could only edit the information when OCR correctly processed the receipt.
Exposing only location information was meaningless to users who had already visited the place.
Difficulty in pinpointing the root cause of the error.
Design Goal
I changed the design goal as follows to reflect known problems.
Premise
When the reliability and quality of location information are improved, users enjoy using location-based services more.
Hypothesis
Standardizing and collecting various information that already exists in paper receipts improves the reliability and quality of place information.
How might I help users to accurately input information during the receipt verification process to boost user engagement?
Perspectives
To prove my hypothesis,
I focused my research on the following four perspectives .
The research helped us identify our key priorities for this round: sharing, multiple lists, and enhanced editing functions.
01
Visibility

Final Design
01
Visibility
1. Emphasize matched place name, address, and date information at the top of the screen.
2. Improved button labels for clearer actions.
"No, I will look for another place" button was misunderstood, and brought users to unintended pages.
So, the edit button was placed just below the location information to enhance clarity.
Renamed the button to "Submit Receipt" to clarify the action users need to take.
02
Better guidelines for receipt scanning
Relocated the instructions to be more visible and emphasized the core information that needs to be scanned.
03
Identifying different error cases
Provide guides for different mismatch scenarios to help users rescan their receipt and prevent leaving the page.
04
Customizing information to drive lock-in effect
I thoroughly reviewed OCR raw data to find out what information was collected. Then, I selected relevant information for users and exposed them to the platform.
